Miley Cyrus mimics Shakira and takes revenge on Liam Hemsworth with new song Flowers

 Posted on July 9, 2024  |  2 minutes  |  406 words  |  Reinaldo Massengill

Miley Cyrus not only delivered her latest song, but has also given the gossip columnists plenty to talk about this weekend. The singer announced a few weeks ago the release of her new album, Endless Summer Vacation, scheduled for March 10, and the single Flowers, serves as a preview for her upcoming release.

The video for the new song sees the singer taking a shower, totally naked, and can be heard repeating the phrase "I can love me better".

Miley Cyrus lyrics about Liam Hemsworth

While the song could be a possible ode to self-love, what should surprise many are the darts Cyrus threw at her ex-husband, actor Liam Hemsworth, in what could only be described as an homage to Shakira.

In this new song, the singer effectively sings about love for oneself and the importance of personal wellbeing. The lyrics are accompanied by a video that shows the singer, who has just faced a presumed break-up and who realises a great truth: she can live and thrive on her own.

Cyrus takes the opportunity to make the aforementioned fact clear with a very meaningful verse:

Miley Cyrus Flowers lyrics

"I can buy myself flowers/Write my name in the sand/Talk to myself for hours/Yeah, things you don't understand", Cyrus sings. "I can take myself dancing, yeah/I can hold my own hand/Yeah, I can love myself better than you can".

The verse takes on greater significance due to two small details: the song is a direct response to Bruno Mars' When I Was Your Man, a song that Liam Hemsworth dedicated to his then-wife; and that Cyrus dropped the bombshell on the actor's birthday.

The song follows Shakira's latest release with Bizarrap where the Colombian singer's lyrics are also said to be perceived digs at her ex Gerard Pique.

The year of Miley

Endless Summer Vacation comes after a great professional year for Cyrus, who in March last year surprised her fans by releasing Attention: Miley Live, her first live album, made up of some of her best hits, covers of well-known classics and unreleased songs.

Produced by Mike WiLL Made-It, Kid Harpoon, Greg Kurstin and Tyler Johnson, the album will be released on March 20 and anticipation is at an all-time high, promising to be one of the albums of the year.

However, if all the tracks are as incendiary as this one, the controversy is going to be more than served for a long time to come.
