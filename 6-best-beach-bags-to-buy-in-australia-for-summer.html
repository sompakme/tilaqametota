WHAT MATERIALS ARE IDEAL FOR BEACH BAGS?

Anything that is quick drying is a massive plus – think fabrics like neoprene, canvas or towelling as well as sturdier materials like straw.

Woven-style bags can be great too as you’re less likely to take the beach home with you at the end of the day – simply give the bag a shake as you’re leaving.

WHAT’S THE BEST SIZE FOR A BEACH BAG?

Well, that’s going to depend on you! But think about what you like to normally bring to the beach.

A beach bag that’s roomy enough to fit a towel, water bottle, sunscreen, hat and some snacks is ideal.

If you’re packing for kids or more than one person it pays to get out a measuring tape and check the bag you’re eyeing up will fit everything you need to bring with you to have a good time.

HOW DO YOU GET SAND OUT OF A BEACH BAG?

The first step is making sure you keep as much sand out of your bag to begin with, so give your towel and other beach gear a good shake before you put them inside the bag.

When you get home empty the bag and give it a good shake – ideally somewhere that’s not going to make another mess you will have to clean up, like in the garden or over a bin.

If there’s wet sand stuck to the bag, turn it inside out and wipe the area down with a damp cloth (no soap needed).
