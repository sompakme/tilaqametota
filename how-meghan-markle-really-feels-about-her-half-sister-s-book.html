How Meghan Markle Really Feels About Her Half-Sister's Book

 Posted on August 10, 2024  |  2 minutes  |  222 words  |  Larita Shotwell

According to an In Touch Weekly source, Meghan Markle "feels sick to her stomach" about Samantha Markle's upcoming book, which is due to be released on Jan. 17, 2021. However, the duchess "won't act bothered or upset about [it] in public."

Samantha told US Weekly on Jan. 13, 2021, that she doesn't intend to "attack" her half-sister, but wants to explain all aspects of growing up with the actress-turned-royal. "Contrary to troll gossip and aggregate news rumors, my book has never been designed to attack my sister," she said. "It's relatable. There's a lot to learn — the good, the bad and the ugly. I've been very forthcoming that I was never writing a hardbound tabloid. I said that two years ago, but nobody wanted to believe it."

Samantha first announced The Diary of Princess Pushy's Sister — Part 1 – which will be published by Barnes & Noble — in 2017. According to Express, Samantha described the book as "fair and balanced," but also noted there will "naturally" be stories that Meghan dislikes. On the other hand, Thomas Markle spoke to US Weekly about the tell-all on Jan. 7, 2021, and said he is "very pleased" with it.

Amid the book drama, sources told In Touch Weekly that Prince Harry is "totally supporting Meghan and doesn't believe a word that comes out of Samantha's mouth."
