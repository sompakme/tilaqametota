Are you getting ready to apply for a job as a millwright? 

Also known as an industrial mechanic, this role involves operating industrial machinery. It is essential to know how to make sure the machinery is properly maintained and be able to operate it safely. It takes certain expertise and training to become a millwright. No matter where you are in your career, you need to show that you have the necessary skills. 

Let’s take an in-depth look at a typical day as a millwright and discover more about this role.

Millwright Job Description

A job description for a millwright should start with an overview of the company you will work for. It should explain the working environment along with the people you will work with. There should also be information about the type of contract, the general shift pattern, and the base salary.

The typical duties that you will be charged with should be explained in the job description. There should also be information on the education, experience, and skills that applicants need to have. There is also likely to be information about the specialist skills and attributes the perfect candidate should have. 

The Working Environment

This job usually takes place in an industrial setting such as a power plant, factory, or construction site. While millwrights usually work around forty hours a week, they typically work shifts. These shifts can include weekends, evenings, and public holidays.

Some millwrights are also on call around the clock in case an emergency or accident occurs. The working environment is usually dirty and noisy, and millwrights work in all types of weather conditions. It is necessary to be able to keep calm and remain focused, and be reasonably physically fit. 

Typical Duties And Responsibilities

There is a range of duties and responsibilities that typically go with this role. Highlighting some of these duties in your resume indicates your experience with the hiring manager. Here are some of the main duties that millwrights are likely to be charged with. 

Installing machine mechanisms

A lot of this job revolves around constructing and installing machine components. This often involves creating machinery from scratch to meet the demands of different tasks. You will also be charged with installing components as parts of a repair job. 

Performing repairs

You need to be skilled at performing a wide range of different types of repairs on heavy machinery. This can range from quick fixes like replacing broken parts to designing new components. It is essential to make sure that repairs are performed as quickly and safely as possible. 

Conducting maintenance

When heavy machinery breaks down during a construction project, it costs employers time and money. Regularly maintaining equipment helps to prevent this from happening. This includes regularly inspecting all components and cleaning them. Associated tasks include lubricating moving parts, replacing worn-out parts, and changing engine fluids. 

Overseeing workers

You are also likely to be charged with monitoring the work of other employees. Be comfortable delegating maintenance tasks and providing on-the-job training. You need to be able to explain different tasks clearly and demonstrate how to perform them. 

Testing machine mechanisms 

You need to be able to perform certain tests on different types of machinery. These tests must be performed before you sign off that the machinery is safe to use. Highlighting this duty shows that you have a good understanding of the job requirements. 

Ensuring projects are completed on time and within budget

This is one of the most important roles in the eyes of the employer. If projects run over time and/or budget, it is likely to cost employers a lot of money. Expressing your expertise with this duty is likely to help you stand out from the crowd. 

Millwright Job Description – Valuable Skills And Attributes

When scanning your resume and application, the hiring manager will be looking for certain skills. Adding these skills to your resume shows that you understand the needs and demands of the job. Here are some of the most important millwright skills and attributes and an overview of their meaning. 

Machining

Highlighting this skill shows that you know how to use a range of machines and tools to create finished products. It includes knowing the correct way to use different types of tools and machines. Associated duties include reading and interpreting blueprints and troubleshooting repair machines. 

Welding

This skill involves joining two pieces of metal together and is often used when building and repairing machinery. It is worth taking the time to learn how to weld cleanly and safely. While it is not technically a traditional millwright duty, welding can be used to secure extra work and income. 

Mechanical drawing

Mechanical drawing is used by millwrights to create and interpret machinery diagrams. This important skill will help you to understand and repair complex machinery. Taking a special course to learn mechanical drawing will give you access to an advanced millwright role. 

Computer-aided design (CAD)

This special software is used to create and modify different types of mechanical designs. While most millwrights excel when it comes to technical skills, they often struggle when it comes to CAD. If you have already mastered this valuable skill, it is important to make sure that your future employer knows it. 

Problem-solving skills

It is essential to be able to resolve any issues that arise quickly and smoothly to prevent holdups. This involves analyzing different options and selecting the best possible solution for the problem. To do this well, you need to be able to keep a cool head in the face of adversity and focus on the issue. 

Technical skills

This job involves a wide range of technical skills that you can highlight on your resume to stand out. These include reading schematics, using precision measurement tools, understanding mechanical systems, and following instructions. The more technical skills you can highlight on your application naturally, the stronger it will be. 

Physical ability 

This job can be physically demanding, and you need to have the stamina and physical fitness to do it. You are likely to remain standing and moving into awkward positions for several hours at a time. You also need to be able to lift and carry heavy tools and move large pieces of equipment. 

Education And Experience

It is necessary to hold a high school diploma or GED to embark on this career path. Many employers specify that millwrights should gain a bachelor’s or associate degree in industrial maintenance or a related field. The job description will specify how much work experience applicants need to have to apply. 

No matter where you are in your career, you will be provided with on-the-job training once you start work. This training provides instruction on the company’s specific procedures and processes. Depending on the job and company, training typically lasts from a couple of weeks to a few months. 

Valuable Millwright Certifications

Earning a specialist certification can give you access to an advanced millwright position. This shows hiring managers that you are serious about your career and have gained specialist skills. Here are some professional certifications that are ideal for millwrights. 

EPA Amusement Operators Safety Certification 

This equipment safety program is offered by the Event Planners Association and provides comprehensive instruction on best risk management practices. This certification is ideal for entry-level candidates and shows employers that you understand how to avoid high-risk issues. 

Rigging Equipment Inspector 

Anyone likely to work with cranes during their daily duties is sure to benefit from this professional certification. It is offered by the Crane Institute Certification to people who have at least two years of work experience. 

The certification is offered under ASME and OSHA requirements and covers all types of rigging equipment. It is necessary to sit and pass an exam to gain certification, and it needs to be renewed every two years. 

Certified Maintenance Manager 

This advanced certification is offered by the American Hotel and Lodging Education Institute and demonstrates your strong management skills. Applicants need to possess sound judgment, management expertise, and practical skills to pass the exam. It is necessary to renew the certification every five years to show that your skills are up to date. 

Fluid Power Industrial Hydraulic Technician 

This professional certification demonstrates your understanding of fluid power theory and related knowledge. Certification is offered by the International Fluid Power Society, and it is necessary to have at least two years of work experience. After sitting an oral and written exam, you need to renew this certification every five years.

Millwright Job Description – Final Thoughts

The secret to landing the millwright job you have set your sights on is reading the job description carefully. Take note of the job requirements and make sure that you meet them. 

You then need to demonstrate how your skills perfectly meet these requirements with your application and resume. Highlighting keywords from the job description in your application is the best way to do this. These keywords will stand out to hiring managers and will show that you have done your homework. 

It is also a good idea to do as much research into the company and specific role as possible before applying.
