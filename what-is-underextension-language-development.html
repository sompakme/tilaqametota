What is Underextension language development?

Unintentional underextension happens when a categorical term (a phrase used to define a collection of things) is misused in language by referring to it as if it only applied to one item rather than all of the objects that belong to that category. This is a common occurrence in youngsters while they are first learning and improving their linguistic skills.

When a youngster employs a regular morpheme in a term that is irregular, this is referred to as overregularization. Plurals and the past tense are the most often seen morphemes that have been overregularized. Saying mouses instead of mice is an example of using the plural form. Also, it’s important to understand what an Underextension is.

Underextension occurs when a youngster learns a term for something but does not use it to refer to other objects in the same category after learning it. For example, a youngster could learn that the family pet is referred to as a dog, but he or she might not realise that the pets in other houses are also referred to as dogs. Overextension is the polar opposite of underextension.
