A look at Big Scarr's net worth as Memphis rapper dies at 22

 Posted on September 3, 2024  |  3 minutes  |  467 words  |  Martina Birk

HITC takes a look at Big Scarr’s 2022 net worth after the confirmed reports of the Memphis rapper’s death at the age of 22.

Scarr’s passing was confirmed by rapper and record executive Gucci Mane, whose label 1017 Global Music, Big Scarr was signed to.

“This hurt,” Gucci captioned an Instagram post alongside carousel of images of the young rapper. “I’m a miss you @bigscarr.”

Many fans are saddened about Scarr who has inspired a lot of people in the hip-hop community with his story and career background.

As fellow artists and fans pay their respects to the musician, we find more about Scarr, including his net worth and beginnings in the rap industry.

Big Scarr’s net worth 2022

According to several reports, Big Scarr had an estimated net worth of $1.2 million as of 2022.

Since making a debut in the rap industry, Scarr landed a major label deal on Gucci’s New 1017/Atlantic Records and gained over 300 million views on his YouTube channel.

His 2021 highlight included peaking at number one on Rolling Stone’s Breakthrough 25 Chart, while this year he earned a spot in XXL 2022 Freshman Class.

Scarr was set to perform at fellow Memphis rapper’s Key Glock’s 2023 Glockoma Tour, which will begin in March next year.

The rapper was also set to appear at Rolling Loud LA next year, which will see appearances from Future, Lil Wayne and Travis Scott.

His early beginning and music career

Scarr was encouraged to release music by his friend and fellow rapper Baby K. He dropped his debut track Make A Play in 2019.

The video gained 50,000 views on YouTube within one week and became one of the songs that shaped Scarr’s career and helped him gain a loyal fan base.

After getting signed on Gucci’s record label in 2020, Scarr dropped his debut mixtape Big Grim Reaper in April 2021. The project featured appearances from Gucci, Pooh Shiesty and Foogiano.

Scarr released a deluxe version of his mixtape, titled Big Grim Reaper: The Return, in February 2022 with appearances from Offset and Queez Ruthless.

The rapper performed at the Parking Lot Concert series in Atlanta in October 2020 and at Hot 107.9 Birthday Bash in 2021.

Tributes paid to Scarr

Many artists and fans are saddened about Scarr’s sudden death and many have shared emotional tributes on social media.

Fellow 1017 label mate Enchanting shared several posts about the late Memphis rapper. She wrote in one Instagram Story: “My baby, my best friend, my son, I love you forever & I’m so sorry.”

“Our thoughts and prayers go out to his friends and family during this tragic time,” wrote a media outlet on Twitter.

“Damn, RIP Big Scar. He was one my favorite out the new generation. Prayers to his family,” said DJ Trap-A-Holics in another tribute.
